The Indian Ecommerce Orders Data Analysis Dashboard is an interactive business intelligence project developed using Power BI to analyze ecommerce sales performance across different regions,
 product categories, and customer purchasing behaviors in India. The project integrates datasets containing order details and transaction information to generate meaningful insights
  that help understand overall business performance and identify key growth opportunities.

The dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of ecommerce operations through a combination of key performance indicators (KPIs) and visual analytics. 
At the top of the dashboard, KPI cards display important metrics including Total Sales (2M), Total Quantity Sold (5615), Total Profit (37K), and Average Order Amount (291.85). 
These indicators provide a quick snapshot of the company’s sales performance and profitability.

To analyze product performance, the dashboard includes a Sales by Category visualization that highlights revenue distribution across major categories such as Electronics, 
Clothing, and Furniture. This helps identify which product categories contribute the most to overall sales. 
A detailed Sub-Category analysis further breaks down sales and profit by specific products such as phones, printers, chairs, sarees, and tables, 
allowing deeper insight into product-level performance.

Customer payment preferences are analyzed through the Sales, Quantity, and Profit by Payment Mode visualization. 
This chart compares different payment methods such as Cash on Delivery (COD), EMI, Credit Card, UPI, and Debit Card, helping businesses understand
 which payment options generate the highest sales and profitability.

Geographical sales patterns are explored through the Sales by State map, which highlights revenue distribution across different states in India. Additionally, 
the Top 10 Cities by Sales treemap identifies high-performing cities such as Indore, Pune, Mumbai, Mathura, and Hyderabad, providing insights into regional demand.

Interactive filters allow users to dynamically explore the data by category, sub-category, payment mode, city, and state. Overall, 
this dashboard demonstrates how tools like MySQL, Python, Excel, and Power BI can transform raw ecommerce data into actionable business insights that support data-driven decision-making.
